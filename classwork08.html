Manatees

All about this threatened species!

Manatees, also known as sea cows, are large, fully aquatic, mostly herbivorous marine mammals. The three types of manatees are the West African manatee, the West Indian manatee, and the Amazonian manatee. Manatees are very peaceful animals that spend most of their time munching on plants.

Manatees usually weigh about 1,210 pounds and can grow to about 9.8 feet in length. They have large paddle-like flippers and a large upper lip used for collecting food. They spend 50% of the day sleeping underwater but must come up for air every 20 minutes.

The main cause of death for manatees are human-related issues. These include habitat destruction and human objects, like boats. Because manatees are slow moving creatures that usually occupy shallow waters, they are often struck by boat propellers causing deep cuts and sometimes even death. Fishing nets and lines also pose as a threat to manatees. However, in recent years, the West Indian manatee has been moved down from the endangered species list to the threatened species list due to habitat improvements and the reduction of human-related threats.
